# Color System

## Palette

- `--color-background` — page background and neutral canvas.
- `--color-surface` — primary surface for cards and panels.
- `--color-surface-alt` — alternate surface for secondary panels.
- `--color-border` — subtle border tone for separation.
- `--color-text` — default text for high readability.
- `--color-muted` — secondary text and supportive labels.
- `--color-primary` — primary brand accent for interactive states.
- `--color-secondary` — supporting accent used for categories.
- `--color-accent` — highlight tone for callouts and legends.
- `--color-success`, `--color-warning`, `--color-danger`, `--color-info` — semantic roles for status and validation.

## Roles

- Primary actions and links use `--color-primary`.
- Secondary labels and badges use `--color-secondary`.
- Accent color is reserved for attention and status highlights.
- Surface colors establish a layered visual hierarchy.

## Accessibility

- Maintain contrast between `--color-text` and `--color-background` for body text.
- Use semantic colors sparingly and document their intended use.
- Avoid relying on color alone to communicate meaning; also use shapes, labels and icons.

## Usage

- Use `--color-surface` for cards, panels and central containers.
- Use `--color-border` for dividers and subtle section boundaries.
- Use `--color-primary-strong` for hover states and strong emphasis.
- Use semantic status colors for badges, annotations and progress indicators.
